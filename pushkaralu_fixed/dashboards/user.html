ℹ️ About Godavari Pushkaralu 2027
🪔 What is Godavari Pushkaralu?

Godavari Pushkaralu is one of South India's grandest religious festivals, celebrated once every 12 years when Jupiter (Brihaspati) transits into the zodiac sign Simha (Leo). Millions of devotees gather on the banks of the sacred Godavari River at Rajahmundry (Rajamahendravaram) to take a holy dip, believing it confers the merit of bathing in 12 sacred rivers over 12 years. The 2027 edition is expected to host 2–3 crore devotees, making it comparable to the Maha Kumbh Mela.

🏛️ Government Preparations
🏗️Akhanda Godavari Tourism Project — comprehensive development covering Havelock Bridge, Pushkar Ghat, Bridgelanka beautification, Kovvur Goshpadakshetram revamp, and Kadiyam Nurseries.
🛖Tent Cities & Homestays — 21 major pilgrimage centres across the state will have tent cities for 2–3 day stays. Traditional manduva logili huts to be leased for rural tourism.
🤖AI-Powered Security — CCTV surveillance with AI crowd-counting and real-time density monitoring across all ghats.
🚌Transport Upgrades — Special APSRTC buses, 311 trains listed through Rajahmundry, and dedicated pilgrim transport corridors.
🏥Medical Infrastructure — Medical camps at every ghat, emergency corridors, and 108 ambulance priority routing.
📍 Location & Getting There

The festival is centred at Rajahmundry (Rajamahendravaram), East Godavari District, Andhra Pradesh — on both banks of the sacred Godavari River.

✈️By Air: Rajahmundry Airport (RJA) — direct flights from Hyderabad, Chennai, Bengaluru
🚂By Train: Rajahmundry Railway Station (RJY) — 311 trains listed, including Pushkaralu Specials
🚌By Road: NH-16 (Kolkata–Chennai Highway) — APSRTC special buses from all major AP/Telangana cities
